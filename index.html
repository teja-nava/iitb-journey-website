My IIT Bombay Journey

When I first stepped into the sprawling and lush green campus of IIT Bombay, I felt a surge of excitement and nervous anticipation. Coming from a smaller town, it was overwhelming to suddenly be surrounded by some of the most brilliant minds in the country, iconic academic buildings, and a culture that values both excellence and freedom. I still remember dragging my suitcase into Hostel 15, the place I’d soon call home for the next few years.

The initial days were filled with discovery — finding lecture halls, attending orientation programs, understanding course credits, and getting to know batchmates who came from all over India. I got lost more than a few times on campus but found help from seniors every step of the way. These early interactions planted the seeds of friendships that I cherish even today.

Over time, I settled into the rhythm of college life. From intense late-night study sessions and early morning classes to weekend hangouts at the lakeside and street food stalls near YP Gate — IIT Bombay has given me a blend of rigorous academics and personal freedom. The journey hasn’t always been smooth; there were tough times when I doubted myself, especially during exam weeks or when project deadlines loomed. But each challenge shaped me, made me more resilient, and taught me the value of persistence.

This website is a heartfelt reflection of that journey. I’ve captured not just milestones and achievements, but also moments of growth, friendship, laughter, and introspection. Through every memory — whether captured in a photo, written in a course notebook, or etched into the walls of my hostel — IIT Bombay has given me a story I’ll proudly carry for life.

Timeline / Milestones

📅 Day 1 - Orientation: I sat in LT auditorium with 1000+ students and faculty. The speeches filled me with awe and pride. That was when it hit me — I’m officially an IITian.
🏠 Hostel Settling: Adjusting to a new room, new roommate, and a new routine — hostel life taught me to be independent, resourceful, and social.
🎉 Mood Indigo: Being a part of MI as a Hospitality Volunteer gave me my first experience of managing large events, dealing with artists, and becoming part of a team larger than myself.
📚 CE213 & CE210: These courses ignited my love for structural and geotechnical engineering. CE213 especially was tough but the labs and real-world applications excited me the most.
💼 First Internship: At a structural consulting firm, I got to apply everything I’d learned in real projects. It was eye-opening and affirmed my choice of civil engineering.
📢 Marketing Coordinator @ E-Cell: Leading Eureka! taught me design, planning, outreach and leadership — my first step into entrepreneurship.

What I Learned

  • 📐 Academic Mastery: The academic rigor of IIT Bombay is unmatched. From complex equations in mechanics to practical lab sessions, I gained deep insights into how engineering solves real problems.
  • 💬 Communication: Seminars, presentations, and marketing work at E-Cell taught me to confidently express my thoughts and pitch ideas.
  • 👥 Teamwork: IITB thrives on collaboration. Whether it was hostel projects, fest volunteering, or club tasks — I learned to work with different people, solve conflicts, and move forward.
  • 🧠 Critical Thinking: Through academic challenges and startup ideation, I developed the ability to think on my feet and approach problems from multiple angles.
  • 🎯 Time Management: With so many parallel activities, I developed a planner mindset. Learning to prioritize has become a life skill I’ll always value.
  • 🌱 Personal Growth: The freedom at IITB helped me understand myself better. I learned when to take breaks, how to stay motivated, and most importantly — how to enjoy the journey, not just the destination.
